C. Interpretation

Based on the scatterplot, for species total count v elevation, there was a clear observation of a negative relationship between the elevation and species richness. Elevations below 500 have higher biodiversity(6-8). Meanwhile, higher elevations of above 500 have 2 or less. Meanwhile, for species total count vs habitat type, most of the OGF are with higher elevation but was observed with 1-5 species, ASG and ESG sites were on the lower elevation but has higher observed species. Meanwhile, CVT habitat type had low species total count even with the varying elevation. Barplots of total species observed per habitat showed that the habitat type “ASG” has the highest cumulative species observed (58), followed by “OGF” (48), then “ESG”(28), and lastly “CVT”(1). The histogram of total species count shows a positively skewed distribution indicating more observation sites are species-poor and there were less sites with high-diversity.
